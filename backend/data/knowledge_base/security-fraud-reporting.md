# Reporting Fraud

## How to Report Fraud

If you believe your NovaPay account has been compromised or you see unauthorized transactions:

**Step 1: Freeze your card immediately**
Go to Cards > select the affected card > Freeze Card. This prevents any further unauthorized transactions.

**Step 2: Report unauthorized transactions**
For each unauthorized transaction:
1. Go to Transactions
2. Tap the unauthorized transaction
3. Tap "Report as Unauthorized"
4. Provide details about why this transaction is fraudulent
5. Submit the report

**Step 3: Contact support**
Chat with Nova or call 1-800-NOVAPAY (available 24/7 for fraud reports). Our fraud team will:
- Review your account for additional unauthorized activity
- Issue a new card with a new number
- Open a fraud investigation

## Investigation Process

**Within 24 hours:** A fraud specialist is assigned to your case. You'll receive a case number via email.

**Within 5 business days:** A provisional credit is issued to your account for the disputed amount while the investigation continues.

**Within 30 days:** The investigation is typically completed. Outcomes:
- **Fraud confirmed:** The provisional credit becomes permanent. A police report may be filed on your behalf.
- **Not fraud:** The provisional credit is reversed. You can appeal within 10 business days.

## What NovaPay Needs From You

- A description of the unauthorized transactions
- When you first noticed the unauthorized activity
- Whether your card was lost, stolen, or still in your possession
- Whether you shared your card details or PIN with anyone
- Any other relevant information (suspicious emails, phone calls, etc.)

## Prevention Tips

- Enable 2FA on your account
- Use unique, strong passwords
- Never share your PIN, password, or verification codes
- Monitor your transaction notifications regularly
- Report suspicious activity immediately — the sooner you report, the faster we can act
