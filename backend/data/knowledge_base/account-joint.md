# Joint Accounts

## Overview

Nova joint accounts allow two people to share a single account with equal access to funds, cards, and features. Joint accounts are ideal for couples, roommates, or business partners who need to manage shared expenses.

## How to Open a Joint Account

1. One person must have an existing Nova account in good standing
2. Go to Settings > Account > Add Joint Account Holder
3. Enter the second person's email address — they will receive an invitation
4. The second person must complete identity verification (same KYC process as opening a new account)
5. Once verified, both account holders have full access

## Joint Account Features

- **Shared balance:** Both holders can view and manage the full account balance
- **Individual cards:** Each holder receives their own debit card (virtual and physical)
- **Equal permissions:** Both holders can make purchases, transfers, and change settings
- **Individual PINs:** Each holder sets their own PIN and 2FA
- **Shared transaction history:** All transactions are visible to both holders
- **Individual spending limits:** Each holder can set personal daily spending limits

## Limits

Joint accounts follow the same limits as the account type (Standard or Premium), but limits are shared — not doubled. For example, if you have a Premium account with a $25,000 daily transfer limit, that limit applies to both holders combined.

## Removing a Joint Account Holder

Either holder can request removal of the other holder by contacting support. The removed holder's cards are deactivated, and they lose access to the account. A 7-day notice period applies before removal takes effect.

## Important Notes

- Both holders are equally responsible for any negative balance or overdraft
- Either holder can close the joint account (with the 14-day cooling-off period)
- Nova does not mediate disputes between joint account holders
